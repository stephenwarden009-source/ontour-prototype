# OnTour — How Tickets Actually Work

## Overview

OnTour is a fan-facing transparency layer for live events.

It is designed to help music fans understand:

* the scale of major tours
* the reality of ticket demand
* their likelihood of getting tickets
* and what it actually takes to put a global tour on the road

This prototype explores a new approach to ticketing clarity in a high-demand, low-trust environment.

---

## The Problem

Ticket buying for major concerts has become:

* opaque
* frustrating
* emotionally charged

Fans often feel:

* the system is rigged
* bots control access
* prices are arbitrary
* they never had a real chance

At the same time, the industry faces real constraints:

* finite supply
* extreme demand
* high production costs
* complex logistics
* thin margins in some areas

OnTour sits in the middle of this tension.

---

## The Idea

Instead of trying to “fix” pricing or eliminate scarcity, OnTour:

* makes demand visible
* explains access pathways
* shows the scale of tours
* provides context before and during ticket sales

The goal is not to remove frustration — but to reduce confusion and distrust.

---

## What This Prototype Shows

This PWA prototype includes:

### 1. Tour Announcement Pages

* Scale of the tour (dates, markets, tickets)
* Demand outlook
* What fans should expect before on-sale

### 2. “Your Chances” Module

* Modeled fan-to-ticket ratios
* Estimated probability ranges
* Strategy paths (lottery, loyalty, market, last-minute)

### 3. Track Record

* Historical tour data
* Sell-out behavior
* Added shows and expansion patterns

### 4. Inside the Tour

* Crew size
* logistics footprint
* production scale
* preparation timeline

---

## Philosophy

OnTour is built on a simple idea:

> Fans don’t just want access — they want understanding.

Transparency does not remove scarcity, but it can make it feel less arbitrary.
